Office Conversation Practice

Anna: Hey Mike, did you finalize the presentation for tomorrow’s meeting?

Mike: Almost. I need to clarify a few statistics in the last section.

Sarah: I noticed some discrepancies in the revenue chart. Did you double-check the figures?

Mike: Not yet. I’ll verify them after lunch.

Anna: We should ensure accuracy before we present. The director is very meticulous.

Sarah: Agreed. We don’t want any mistakes or confusion.

Mike: True. I’ll make the adjustments and update you both by 3 PM.

Anna: Perfect. Let’s make sure everything is ready before the final review.

Sarah: Sounds good. We’ll go through everything at 4 PM.

Mike: Alright, see you then!

Pronunciation Practice: Rising & Falling Intonation

In English, the way your voice rises or falls can completely change how natural and clear you sound. This exercise will help you practice using intonation to speak more fluidly and confidently.

↘️ Part 1: Falling Intonation (Statement, Command, or WH-Question)

These examples come from real office conversations and reflect confident, complete thoughts.

  • I need to clarify a few statistics. (Falling – Statement)
  • The director is very meticulous. (Falling – Statement)
  • We should ensure accuracy before we present. (Falling – Statement)
  • What time is the final review? (Falling – WH- Question)
  • Please update us by 3 PM. (Falling – Command)

↗️ Part 2: Rising Intonation (Yes/No Question or Uncertainty)

These questions reflect how professionals check details or express uncertainty in a conversation.

  • Did you finalize the presentation for tomorrow's meeting? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
  • Have you double-checked the figures? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
  • You haven't verified the statistics yet? (Rising – Uncertainty)
  • Is everything ready for the final review? (Rising – Clarification)
  • Should we go through everything at 4 PM? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
Helpful Tip:

• ↘️ Falling Intonation = shows confidence, finality, or a complete idea.

• ↗️ Rising Intonation = shows a question, uncertainty, or a request for confirmation.

• Practicing intonation helps you sound more fluent, natural, and professional in any conversation.
